Coding

Even before high school I was playing with Visual Basic. While I didn't get too much farther than "Hello World," I discovered that the straightforward, systematized nature of coding meshed perfectly with my personality. After that came some time with HTML and Javascript, but I never took it to the career level. I would help friends out when they wanted to spruce up an existing website, or even build websites with WordPress, but it was always side projects.

Now I'm ready to make this a true career and to dive deep into a tech field. I've always wanted to do this for a living, and I'm grateful that I now have the opportunity.

Cooking

I love good food! I'm a trained chef and have worked for numerous high end restaurants. Anything from French to Thai to Japanese is fair game for me. Espscially when they can all be combined!

While I do love to cook and working in restaurants is always exciting, it's not particularly good for raising a family. It's also not a strong skill for helping people beyond just a meal or two.

Learning how to talk to people and getting some hands on management experience has been invaluable and I look forward to using those skills in my new career.

Technology

25 years ago, I was 11 years old working on a old Intel 386 computer running the absolute latest version of Windows 3.1, connecting to CompuServe with a 14.4 Kb/s modem, and pushing a blazing 4 KB of Ram.

12 years ago, I was saving up for a strange new phone that Apple was going to release. Supposedly, you could access the actual internet, play music, AND talk all on the same device!

Currently, one of the goals I have with learning to code for a career is being able to explore quantum computing. For my entire life, I've wanted to be at the forefront of technology and now I can be!
